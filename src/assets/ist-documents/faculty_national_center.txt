The faculty of the National Centre for Remote Sensing and Geo-Informatics (NCRG) at the Institute of Space Technology (IST) includes several distinguished members.

Muhammad Ateeq Qureshi, who serves as the Head of the Department and is a faculty member of NCRG, holds a PhD from the University of Chinese Academy of Sciences (UCAS) in Beijing, China. His areas of specialization include Geographic Information Systems (GIS), Remote Sensing, and Glacier Geomorphology.

Muhammad Jahanzeb Malik is an Assistant Professor and a faculty member of NCRG. He holds a PhD from the University of Twente (ITC) in Enschede, the Netherlands, and specializes in Microwave and Optical Remote Sensing.

Abdul Basit, a Lecturer and a faculty member of NCRG, holds an MS from the Institute of Space Technology, NCRG Karachi. His area of expertise is Remote Sensing and Geographic Information Science (GISc).

Rao Muhammad Zahid Khalil is also a Lecturer and a faculty member of NCRG. He holds an MS from the Institute of Space Technology, NCRG Karachi, and specializes in Geography, Hydrology, Microwave Remote Sensing, and GIS.

Sajid Majeed, another Lecturer and a faculty member of NCRG, holds an MS from the National University of Computer & Emerging Sciences (NUCES-FAST), Karachi. His areas of specialization include Data Science, Machine Learning, Artificial Intelligence, and Deep Learning.

Each faculty member brings a wealth of knowledge and expertise in their respective fields, contributing significantly to the educational and research missions of NCRG.
