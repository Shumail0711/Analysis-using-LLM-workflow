The Faculty of Avionics Engineering at the Institute of Space Technology (IST) boasts a team of accomplished individuals dedicated to advancing the field. Leading the department is Professor Israr Hussain, who holds a Ph.D. from The University of Manchester, UK, specializing in Signals & Image Processing. Professor Muhammad Abdul Rehman Khan, with a Ph.D. from Beihang University, Beijing, China, specializes in Secure Communications & Information Systems, while Professor Muhammad Amin, a Ph.D. graduate from Queen's University Belfast, UK, focuses on Antennas for Space and Land Communications. Professor Suhail Akhtar, with a Ph.D. from the University of Michigan Ann Arbor, USA, specializes in Flight Dynamics & Control.

Associate Professor Abdul Waheed, also serving as the Director of ORIC, obtained his Ph.D. from The Hong Kong University of Science and Technology, focusing on Modelling Simulation and Control. The department's Assistant Professors include Irfan Majid, specializing in Avionics with an MS from NUST CAE, and Muhammad Zahid Sheikh, with an MS from George Washington University, USA, specializing in Data and Computer Communications. Sardar Ahmed, an Assistant Professor with an MS from NUST Islamabad, specializes in Avionics.

The department's lecturers, including Adeel Yousaf, Anila ali, Hafiz Ali Ahmad, Hamail Sultan, Lal Said, and Shifa Nadeem, bring diverse expertise to the faculty. Adeel Yousaf, pursuing a Ph.D. abroad, specializes in Deep-learning, Machine Learning, Computer Vision, and Image Processing. Anila ali, with an MS from Comsat University Islamabad, specializes in Control Systems. Hafiz Ali Ahmad, with an MS from NUST, specializes in Control Systems, Power Electronics, Electric Machines & Drives, and Electric Vehicles. Hamail Sultan, Lal Said, and Shifa Nadeem, all with MS degrees from the Institute of Space Technology, specialize in Wireless Communication Systems, Computer Vision, Pattern Recognition, and Wireless Communication Systems, respectively.

The lab engineers, Maha Intakhab Alam and Rida Nasir, both hold BS degrees in Avionics Engineering from the Institute of Space Technology. Maha specializes in RF & Microwave, Digital Signal Process, and Radar Absorbing Materials, while Rida focuses on Avionics System Design and Radar Systems. Collectively, the faculty of the Department of Avionics Engineering at IST is dedicated to research, teaching, and innovation in the field of avionics, contributing significantly to the advancement of aerospace technology.
